The Challenge

Forest health decline is silent, fast, and hard to catch in time.

You can't be everywhere

Large forest massifs require constant monitoring. But field presence is decreasing — some areas are rarely visited, and threats develop unnoticed between inspections.

By the time you see it, it's too late

Bark beetle infestations and fungal diseases spread rapidly. Visual detection on the ground often comes months after the problem started — when salvage options are already limited.

Prioritizing without data is a gamble

Without objective visibility, every field trip is a bet. You spend time on healthy parcels while critical zones deteriorate elsewhere.

Remote and inaccessible areas fall through the cracks

Steep terrain, poor access roads, understaffed teams — some zones simply don't get visited. Satellite monitoring closes that gap.

FAQ

Common questions.

Does it replace field visits?

No — it makes them more effective. You still go to the field, but only where it matters. No more wasted days on healthy parcels while problems develop elsewhere.

Which conifer species are supported?

Our models are optimized for spruce, with excellent validated performance. Silver fir and Douglas fir work well. For other species (pine, larch), we are still developing reliable detection — contact us to discuss.

What if it is cloudy for several days?

We wait for the next clear satellite image before running the analysis. You do not lose anything — the analysis is performed as soon as conditions allow.

Can I try before committing?

Yes. We provide a free retrospective analysis of your parcels over 12 months at no cost — so you can validate detection quality on your own territory before any commitment.

How do I share my parcels?

By GIS file (Shapefile, GeoJSON or GeoPackage) sent by email. Your account is configured within 48 hours.
