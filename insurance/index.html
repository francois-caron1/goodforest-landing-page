The Challenge

Forest damage is hard to see, harder to measure — and every delay has a cost.

Whether after a storm or a wildfire.

You don't know where to look first

After a major storm like Nils (France, 2026), dozens of claims land simultaneously. Without visibility on actual damage, every parcel feels urgent. Experts get mobilized where damage is limited — while the critical zones wait.

The clock is ticking on your timber

Fast-degrading species like spruce and maritime pine need rapid assessment. Post-Ciaran (France, 2023), some expertises took up to 6 months. The longer the wait, the harder it is to salvage and sell the timber — and to close the claim cleanly.

When access is impossible, data becomes critical

After a wildfire, ground access can be blocked for weeks. Satellite assessment delivers precise damage rates and affected zones — without requiring an expert on site.

Disputed claims need objective ground truth

When damage figures are contested, a satellite-based assessment from a trusted third party gives everyone — insurer and owner — a shared, independent reference point.

FAQ

Common questions.

How long after the event can you deliver results?

Delivery time depends on satellite revisit frequency and cloud cover over the affected area. Results are typically available within a few weeks post-event — significantly faster than traditional on-site expert assessment. We'll give you an honest timeline estimate as soon as you submit your perimeter.

What is the spatial resolution of your analysis?

Our analysis reaches a granularity of 100 m². This allows meaningful plot-level differentiation — comparable in precision to what an on-site expert would document.

Is it compatible with my existing claims workflow?

Yes. We adapt to your existing workflow and report format. API integration is available for teams who want to connect Goodforest directly to their claims management systems. Contact us to discuss your setup.
